Additional info

All those little things you need to know before giving us your money.

Which rate should I pay?

The short answer

If you are paying your own way to the conference, select Individual. If your employer is paying for you to attend DjangoCon, select Corporate. This is a community-supported conference; please be honest when choosing a ticket type.

Corporate rate

If you have your own company or your employer is paying for your trip (which he should): please opt for the corporate rate. Included in your ticket purchase is the warm fuzzy feeling of knowing you enabled more students to afford their attendance fee and food for the month.

Individual rate

If you use Django for personal projects and want to attend the conference on your own expense. In that case: the individual rate is all you ever wanted.

Student rate

The student rate is for full-time students only and is limited in number. Students who have bought a ticket should email us a scan of a valid student ID as proof within 48 hours or risk their ticket being cancelled.

Academics and employees of non-profits should register at the normal rates, depending on whether you are paying your own way to the conference (see above).

What you're getting

Registration for the conference includes:

  • Access to all talks and sessions
  • Free lunch
  • Coffee and snacks during breaks
  • Free wifi at the conference
  • And... a beautiful t-shirt, of course

Buy tickets early

DjangoCon is a community effort, organised by Djangonauts like you. If you plan to attend the conference (which you do, right?) then please buy your tickets early, it will allow us to get our financial picture clear earlier, which helps the organisation a lot.

Furthermore buying your ticket before April 1st gives you the early bird discount.

Hotel and lodging

We strongly encourage you to book a room at the very moment you book your tickets, see: hotels. If you do so you can make use of the special price and conditions available to us through our DjangoCon room blocks.

The room blocks will expire at: April 1st
